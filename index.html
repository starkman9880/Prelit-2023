Pre-Lit Fest 2023: Henry Threadgill in Conversation with Yusef Komunyakaa

Wednesday, June 14 | 6:30 PM | Schomburg Center
Join us for an event celebrating the publication of Easily Slip into Another World: A Life in Music, the autobiography of composer and multi-instrumentalist Henry Threadgill, co-written with Columbia University professor and director of the Schomburg Center’s Scholars-in-Residence program Brent Hayes Edwards. The evening will feature a reading and conversation with Threadgill and the poet Yusef Komunyakaa, the two Black Vietnam veterans who have been awarded the Pulitzer Prize, as they reflect upon their experiences in the war and its impact on their long careers as artists.

Chevalier de Saint-Georges, One of the First Known Composers of African Descent

The release of the film Chevalier has brought attention to Joseph Bologne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges (1745–1799). Bologne is one of the first known composers of African descent. He influenced peers of his day such as Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart and inspired authors including Alexandre Dumas. As the Center celebrates Black Music Month, discover materials in the collections about the composer—including writings from our founder, Arturo Schomburg.

Register for Exhibition Tours! Marking Time: Art in the Age of Mass Incarceration

Through Monday, December 4 | Schomburg Center
Free 45-minute tours of Marking Time: Art in the Age of Mass Incarceration are now available on select Mondays between June and August. Spanning three galleries across the Schomburg Center, Marking Time explores the impact of the U.S. prison system on contemporary visual art. This exhibition highlights 40 artists who are or have been incarcerated alongside artists who have not been incarcerated but whose practices interrogate the carceral state. Curated by Dr. Nicole R. Fleetwood, James Weldon Johnson Professor of Media at NYU, 2021 MacArthur Fellow, and former Schomburg Center Scholar-in-Residence fellow.
